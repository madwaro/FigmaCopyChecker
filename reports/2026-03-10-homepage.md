### Issues

#### 🔴 High — Date Format Hardcoded
**Element:** "Posted on 02/18/2026", "Modified on 02/18/2026", "Posted on 02/17/2026", "Received on 02/14/2026"
**UI Type:** Label / timestamp
**Rule:** 8.4 — Don't hardcode date/time formats
**Issue:** Dates use numeric US format `MM/DD/YYYY` which is ambiguous globally. Should use locale-aware formatting or a clearer format like "Feb 18, 2026".
**Current text:** "Posted on 02/18/2026"
**Suggested fix:** "Posted on Feb 18, 2026" (or dynamically locale-formatted)

---

#### 🔴 High — Possible Content Bug (Date Inconsistency)
**Element:** "Update your iOS device by January 20 to maintain secure access to company systems."
**UI Type:** Body text (notification card)
**Rule:** 1.4 — Don't mislead the user
**Issue:** The card is "Posted on 02/17/2026" but the deadline referenced is "January 20" — already in the past. This is either a content bug or the card should have been dismissed.
**Current text:** "Update your iOS device by January 20 to maintain secure access to company systems."
**Suggested fix:** Verify the deadline date is correct. If past, remove or update the card.

#### 🟡 Medium — Lead with What's Important
**Element:** "Update your iOS device by January 20 to maintain secure access to company systems."
**UI Type:** Body text (notification card)
**Rule:** 4.2 — Lead with what's most important; front-load key info
**Issue:** The deadline is buried mid-sentence. Leading with the date makes it more scannable.
**Current text:** "Update your iOS device by January 20 to maintain secure access to company systems."
**Suggested fix:** "By January 20, update your iOS device to keep access to company systems."

#### 🔵 Low — Scannability of Notification Cards
**Element:** Multiple notification cards in "Stay up to date" section
**UI Type:** Body text

- **"Connect to a live agent"** — warm and helpful phrasing, aligned with the "ready to lend a hand" voice principle.
- **"Quick access"** heading — short, scannable, correct capitalization.
- **Chat history items** ("What's the policy for relocation?", "How to set up VPN on my new iPhone?") — natural, conversational tone with appropriate contractions.
- **Data labels** ("PTO days remaining", "Perks+ budget available", "Next paid holiday", "HSA spending available") — concise, no periods, sentence-style caps. Great scannability.
- **Tab labels** ("Benefits", "Travel", "IT Helpdesk", "News", "Paystub") — clean, consistent, no punctuation.
- **Chat input placeholder** ("Ask Copilot") — sentence-style caps, no period, clear purpose.
- **"Submit time off"** button — active voice, action-oriented, concise.

### 💡 General recommendations
1. **Date consistency:** Switch all dates to locale-aware rendering or a human-friendly format (e.g., "Feb 18, 2026") per rule 8.4.
2. **Verify deadlines:** The device update card references "January 20" but was posted on Feb 17 — likely a content bug.
3. **Tone:** Overall copy is strong — conversational, brief, and well-structured. The main areas to address are date formatting and one exclamation point.
4. **Notification cards:** Consider standardizing card description length (1 sentence preferred) to improve scan speed across the "Stay up to date" section.
